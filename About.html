Cricket

Objective: The primary aim of the game is to score more runs than the opposing team. This is achieved by hitting the ball with a bat and running between two sets of wooden stumps, called wickets, situated at either end of the 22-yard-long pitch. Playing Equipment: Ball: A hard leather ball, usually red in Test cricket and white in limited-overs formats like One Day Internationals (ODIs) and Twenty20 (T20) matches. Bat: A flat, wooden bat used by batsmen to hit the ball. Wickets: Three wooden stumps, topped by two bails, form a wicket. These are placed at each end of the pitch. Protective Gear: Batsmen wear protective gear such as helmets, pads, gloves, and abdominal guards to prevent injury from the hard ball. Playing Positions: Batsmen: Two batsmen are on the field at any given time. Their primary job is to score runs. Bowlers: Bowlers deliver the ball to the batsmen with the aim of getting them out or limiting the number of runs scored. Fielders: The other players on the team are fielders. They aim to stop the ball and dismiss batsmen through catches, run-outs, or stumping.
